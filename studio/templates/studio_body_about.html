iGLou Studio is the denomination for the freelance activity of Jonathan Boucher. While the label studio is usually used in a more-than-one-people structure, we'd like to emphasis with this naming the multidisciplinary skills gathered at the same place. Developer, designer, manager, trainer, advisor are as many as different hats we could wear to deliver a wild range of services.

The studio activity is shared between subcontracting app development and producing its own ones.

We love crafting custom-made software

Uniqueness quantification is the way it's mean to consider each project at iGLou Studio. In other words, through a permanent discussion with our partners, each project starts with its own custom basis to enable the delivery of a well-craft solution meeting expectations as fully as possible. Consequently, there's no magic behind: courage, patience and dialogue.

Community & Sharing

Even from a business point-of-view, locking knowledge and techniques is a really old-school «Microsoftish» approach for all digital-technology-related activities. Today Sharing & Community is the key to succeed, leading to a win-win scenario for each party. Following this idea, we will provide and feed continuously an educational dedicated section as a spin-off of iGLou::Studio activities, under the iGLou::Forges label.

Open Project

The door stay open at iGLou::Studio for open project as well as for charity or non-profit organization ones.

MAD SCIENTIST

Few years ago my activity was consisting in torturing silicon wafers, shattering their surface atoms with an highly-accelerated ion beam before burning them in oven with temperature ramp over a million degree per second. The electrical behavior of such generated nanometric scale defects obtained by a transient spectroscopy method (so by electrocution) was my PhD thesis subject.

GTJ: Great Teacher Jo.

During three years I could practice teaching through a university teacher fellowship from undergraduate to master level at Toulouse in department of Physics, Paul-Sabatier Science University.

PROCESS ENGINEER

After a rich academical experience, I entered the world of production as process engineer in a semiconductor foundry of a well-known international company. Lean management in action is really impressive! S5 methods should never be forgotten:

SELF MADE DEV

Except the binary math leaning and few algorithmic and numerical method lessons of my initial physicist formation, my only advanced computer related curse was the excellent one of Mathias Paulin about real-time rendering at master level from the toulouse university computer department.
